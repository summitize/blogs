ChatGPT - An Astrology Champ

During the recent T20 World Cup, I ran a small but fascinating experiment with AI. Instead of using ChatGPT as a simple information tool, I used GPT Astro, a version of ChatGPT that interprets events through Vedic astrological principles, to track the entire tournament.

Rather than asking for a one-time prediction such as “Who will win the World Cup?”, I treated the AI like a live analyst. I continuously interacted with it during matches, providing match context, toss decisions, and real-time score updates to see how the analysis evolved as the game unfolded.

This experiment wasn't about fortune telling — it was about observing how AI can interpret patterns and momentum when fed structured, real-time inputs.

The Setup: Treating AI as a Live Analyst

The interaction began with broad prompts asking questions like:

  • Which teams had the strongest astrological alignment to win the tournament?
  • Which teams were most likely to reach the semifinals?
  • What planetary patterns might influence knockout matches?

Once the tournament started, the experiment became far more interactive. Instead of static questions, I began sharing live match situations.

  • Match start times
  • Toss decisions
  • Score updates during the innings
  • Momentum changes during key overs

Typical prompts looked like this: 


67/1 after 6 overs. How does the astrology look now?
170/4 in the chase. Does momentum shift?
NZ 52/3 after powerplay chasing 255.

Instead of simply predicting winners, GPT Astro interpreted phases of the match using concepts such as:

  • Mars energy representing aggression and explosive scoring
  • Moon instability affecting early match volatility
  • Sun authority representing championship momentum

How the Predictions Evolved

One of the strongest aspects of the interaction was how predictions changed as the match progressed. GPT Astro continuously updated its outlook based on the latest information.

Certain patterns appeared repeatedly:

  • Powerplay volatility — early overs were often unstable.
  • Middle overs control — overs 7–15 usually decided momentum.
  • High total pressure — when teams crossed 220+, chasing sides often collapsed under pressure.

Rather than claiming certainty, the AI described likely match trajectories.

If the batting side crosses 200, the chasing team may start aggressively but lose wickets in the middle overs due to pressure.

The Semifinal Test Case

The India vs England semifinal became a real test of the approach.

As the match unfolded, I shared continuous score updates with GPT Astro. India produced a huge total, and England began an aggressive chase. For a while, the match looked balanced.

However, GPT Astro suggested that the 14–16 over window was a pressure phase where wickets were likely. Eventually, England lost momentum and fell short, confirming the predicted match pattern.

The Final: India vs New Zealand

Before the final began, GPT Astro suggested India had a slight astrological edge, although the toss could influence the match.

New Zealand won the toss and chose to field. Then the game began explosively.

  • India scored 51 runs in the first four overs
  • They crossed 150 runs before the twelfth over

With each score update, the AI's confidence increased. Once India crossed 150 with only one wicket down, GPT Astro projected a massive total.

India eventually finished with 255 runs — one of the highest totals ever in a T20 World Cup final.

The Chase and the Result

During New Zealand’s chase, the AI predicted that the enormous required run rate would force aggressive batting, increasing the probability of wickets.

That is exactly what happened. New Zealand lost multiple wickets in the powerplay while trying to keep up with the scoring rate.

India eventually won the World Cup.

What Made This Experiment Interesting

  • Continuous adaptation – predictions changed with every score update.
  • Pattern-based reasoning – the AI focused on phases of the match.
  • Interactive analysis – each prompt triggered a new interpretation.

Final Thoughts

The goal of the experiment was never to prove astrology. Instead, it was to explore what happens when AI is used as an interactive analysis partner during live events.

With every score update, the conversation became a dynamic interpretation of patterns, probabilities, and momentum.

Sometimes the most interesting insights come not from the final prediction, but from the journey of analysis along the way.
